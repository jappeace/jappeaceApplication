Core tenets

In my work I abide to several Core tenets which drive my technical decisions.

Simplicity

The quality or condition of being plain or uncomplicated in form or design.

Therefore reason I follow this tenent is purely pragmatic. I want to be able to understand my own code after seeing it several months later easily. A happy side affect is that other engineers read my code also more easily, therefore I become more replacable.

Robustness

The ability to withstand or overcome adverse conditions or rigorous testing.

The reason I want robustness is more a matter of pride. I really don't appreciate code I wrote breaking on the job. Therefore I will design with robustness in mind. I wish to build things that can stand the test of time and outlive me many times over. This restriction also drives me to search for ever more powerfull tools.

Elegance

the quality of being graceful and stylish in appearance or manner.

the quality of being pleasingly ingenious and simple; neatness.

Ugly systems are hard to work with and demotivating, therefore I think elegance is something to be strived for. Elegance goes with simplicity nicely. This is by far the hardest to do right, and usually involves several iterations. I believe this includes an elegant user interface, although this is even harder.
